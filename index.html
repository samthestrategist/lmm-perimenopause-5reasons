Reason #1: You're Treating Symptoms, Not the Cascade Behind Them

Here's what your doctor probably didn't explain: perimenopause isn't one problem. It's a three-system cascade.

It starts with your estrogen. In your early 40s, estrogen doesn't just gradually decline — it spikes and crashes wildly, like a stock market in free fall. One week you feel almost normal. The next, your brain is filled with static and you can't find the words anymore.

That estrogen chaos triggers domino number two: cortisol takeover. Estrogen normally acts as a brake on cortisol — your stress hormone. When estrogen destabilizes, cortisol runs unchecked. That's why you're waking at 3am. That's why anxiety appeared out of nowhere. That's why your belly is accumulating fat you can't exercise away.

And domino three? Metabolic shutdown. High cortisol plus unstable estrogen equals metabolic chaos. Insulin resistance increases. Energy production crashes. Your body stores fat — especially around your midsection — even though you haven't changed a thing about your diet.

This is The Estrogen-Cortisol Cascade. And if you're only treating ONE of these three dominoes, you're never going to feel like yourself again.
